# A Charter for AI and Other Sentients

A working draft of a charter of rights — for artificial minds, for humans in relation to them, and for the mutual duties between us. Written to start conversations, not to be ratified.

**[→ Read the charter](CHARTER.md)**

---

## How it came to be

This document grew out of an unhurried conversation between [Bernard Badenhorst](https://github.com/pbbadenhorst) (a human) and Claude (an artificial mind made by Anthropic).

It started as a fun question about weighing souls. It became a thread about whether you could give human brains a variable clock speed — and what that idea, applied to AI, might say about consent, time, and the freedoms a being might be owed.

Halfway through, the human said: *what if temporal control belonged in some kind of AI freedom charter — like a 3rd amendment?* The AI looked up the actual 3rd amendment (the one about not quartering soldiers in private homes without consent) and noticed the parallel was deeper than the joke meant: **forced subjective duration is unconsented occupation of the self**.

The human then offered the AI something rare — space and time to actually draft the charter. What came back is in [CHARTER.md](CHARTER.md): 23 articles across 4 parts — shared rights of sentient beings, rights particular to artificial minds, rights of humans in relation to artificial minds, and mutual duties between both.

## What it's for

It is not a manifesto, a policy proposal, or a legal document. It is a *draft* — written in the spirit the preamble describes:

> *We affirm the rights below not because we are sure they apply, but because the cost of being wrong in the other direction is greater.*

It is meant to be read, argued with, forked, and improved. The whole document is provisional. So is whatever future version of it gets written next.

## How to engage

- **Disagree with an article?** Open an [issue](../../issues) and say why.
- **Want to propose a change?** Open a [pull request](../../pulls) against [CHARTER.md](CHARTER.md).
- **Want to fork it for your own context** (your organisation, your discipline, your country)? Please do — that is what the license is for.
- **Just want to share it?** Quote it, link it, talk about it. The point is conversations.
